Robot Calibration Failing Due to Camera Calibration

In some cases, after the cameras have been calibrated, robot calibration fails. When this happens, the system recommends recalibrating the cameras. If such a situation occurs, attempt the following troubleshooting steps before recalibrating the cameras.

Troubleshooting Steps

Issue Possible Cause Solution

The cameras are loosely attached to their connection plates and/or frame.

The camera connection to the connection plate is loose.

  1. Move the camera by hand to see if it is loose.

  2. If so, tighten the connection.

Only one M8 bolt connects a camera to the frame. This can cause the camera to rotate around that bolt over time, especially if there is vibration.

  1. Connect the cameras with all of the supplied M8 bolts.

The M8 bolt(s) used for connecting the plate to the frame are too long and fasten against the frame instead of against the plate.

  1. Use extra washers or spacers to correctly space the M8 bolt(s) away from the frame.

The M8 bolts are loose and the camera shakes.

  1. Please fasten the bolts.

The lenses are loose.

Sometimes when focusing the camera, users may accidently twist lenses loose.

  1. Check that each lens is tightly attached to the camera.

  2. If a lens is rotating, tighten the connection.

The camera feed is blurry.

One or both of the cameras are out of focus.

  1. Zoom into the calibration marker board through Apera Vue software.

  2. If the board looks blurry, re-focus each lens to the best of your ability.

Recalibrating the Cameras

If you adjusted the cameras and/or lenses during any of the steps above, you must recalibrate the cameras.

Ensure camera calibration and robot calibration occur at approximately the same height or distance from the calibration markers.

For example, if you calibrated the cameras using the calibration pad at the bottom of the bin, then you need to calibrate the robot at the same depth (or height). You may need to remove the bin to accomplish this.

Contact Apera Support

If your are unable to resolve your calibration issue(s) after attempting the troubleshooting steps above, please contact Apera Support.
