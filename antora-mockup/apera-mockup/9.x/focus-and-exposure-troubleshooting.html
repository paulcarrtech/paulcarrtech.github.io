Focus and Exposure Troubleshooting

Before You Begin

Assumptions

  • Apera Vue Computer is activated and running Apera Vue software (version 8.0.0 or higher)

  • VuePort or custom camera pair has already been calibrated

Before adjusting the lens to improve robot calibration results, double-check whether both cameras have optimal focus and aperture settings.

F# on the lens is usually set at F# 4.

Check for an Over-Exposed Image

If you see an over-exposed image with your lens at F# 4, the exposure time is likely high. This causes white regions of the camera calibration board to saturate.

Example of an over-exposed image.

You can change exposure time in Apera Vue:

  1. Select Cameras > Capture Settings

  2. Select Edit > Edit Exposure Time on the capture you are currently using.

  3. If your image was over exposed, set the exposure time to optimal values.

For indoor environments, we recommend an exposure time of 33 ms to 66 ms.
The same image as above with optiomal exposure. (66 ms).

Lens Focus and F# Settings

The recommended aperture setting for each lens is F# 4.

If the lens was out of focus and/or on a lower F# when you previously performed robot calibration, please refocus the lens and set F# 4.

With the camera calibration board in frame, the focus on both cameras must look similar to the image below.
Correct camera focus on the calibration board at F# 4.

Increase the Lens F# to F# 5.6

  1. Loosen the lock screw on the aperture ring without altering the focus ring of both cameras.

  2. Change the F# from 4 to 5.6 on both lenses.

  3. Recalibrate the cameras.

  4. Recalibrate the robot.

If robot calibration is still failing, contact Apera Support.
