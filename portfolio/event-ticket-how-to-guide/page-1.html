Terminology

Tours
What we refer to as events or public programs are called tours in Centaman.
Ticket Types
Ticket types define the access permissions of printed tickets. Every admission price point (e.g., Adult, Senior, Student) for an individual tour typically share the same ticket type.
Stock Buttons
Stock buttons are the stock-keeping units (SKUs) for each admission price point. Stock buttons associate general ledger (GL) accounts and ticket types to admission price points.
Extra Items
What we refer to as tickets are called extra items in Centaman. Extra items define the price points of admissions. Selling an extra item counts against the crowd capacity: the number of tickets available. Each extra item is associated with a stock button, which in turn associates that extra item with a GL account and a ticket type.
Tour Buttons
Tour buttons allow the cashier to sell admissions to a tour through the point-of-sale (POS) interface. Each tour button is associated to an extra item.
Primary Bookings
Primary bookings define the date and time of a tour.
Secondary Bookings
Secondary bookings are extra items (i.e, admissions) sold for a tour on a specific date and time.
Booking Types
Booking types define which extra items are associated with a tour.
Resources
There are two types of resources used in tours:
  • Room resources attach to primary bookings to avoid room booking clashes.
  • Public program resources control how many extra items (i.e., tickets or admissions) are available for a tour. These resources also push tour buttons (for selling extra items) to the date of the tour within the POS interface.
