Capturing Screenshots with FastStone Capture

You can use FastStone Capture to capture screenshots in a variety of ways.

Before You Start

  1. Download and install the FastStone Capture toolbar (if not already installed).
  2. Open the FastStone Capture toolbar.
  3. Arrange any open program, browser, and/or dialog windows on your desktop in the manner you wish to capture.

Capturing Screenshots

Capture Screenshots in the following ways.

Capturing Active Windows

You can capture the currently active window.

  1. Left-click on a window to make it active.
  2. Left-click on the Capture Active Window button on the FastStone Capture toolbar.

The captured screenshot crops out anything outside of the active window. (See Figure 1.)

Capturing Windows and Objects

  1. Left-click on the Capture Window / Object button on the FastStone Capture toolbar.
  2. Hover your cursor over a window or over an object within a window to highlight that window/object with a red frame.
  3. Choose from the following options:
    • Left-click to capture the highlighted window/object. (See Figure 2.)
    • Hold the CTRL key and left-click on multiple windows/objects to select them all.
    • Hold the SHIFT key to focus on the highlighted windows/objects.
    • Press ENTER to capture the highlighted windows/objects. (See Figure 2.)
    • Press ESC to cancel capturing anything.

Capturing Rectangular Regions

  1. Left-click on the Capture Rectangular Region button on the FastStone Capture toolbar.
  2. Left-click and drag the crosshairs to draw a rectangle over the region of the screen you wish to capture.
  3. Release the left mouse button to capture the region. (See figure 3.)

Capturing Freehand Regions

  1. Left-click on the Capture Freehand Region button on the FastStone Capture toolbar.
  2. Left-click and begin dragging the lasso cursor.
  3. Continue dragging the lasso cursor to draw a selection over the region of the screen you wish to capture.
  4. Drag the lasso cursor to the starting point of the selection to capture the region. (See Figure 4).
